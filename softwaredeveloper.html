Tarantula

Integrated Multi-Dimensional Flow Modeling

Tarantula is a revolutionary software that seamlessly integrates 1D, 2D, and 3D flow motion modeling in a unified computational framework. This powerful tool allows engineers to model complex hydraulic systems with unprecedented flexibility and accuracy.

Key Capabilities:

  • Unified 1D/2D/3D Integration: Seamlessly couple different dimensional models in a single simulation
  • Advanced CFD Algorithms: State-of-the-art numerical methods for accurate flow prediction
  • Multi-Scale Modeling: Efficiently model systems from local details to regional scales
  • Complex Geometries: Handle intricate hydraulic structures and natural features
  • Turbulence Modeling: Multiple turbulence closure schemes for various applications
  • Free Surface Flows: Advanced Volume-of-Fluid (VOF) methods for water surface tracking
  • High-Performance Computing: Parallel processing support for large-scale simulations

Applications:

  • River and channel hydraulics with local 3D details
  • Complex hydraulic structures (spillways, gates, weirs)
  • Urban drainage systems with detailed inlet/outlet modeling
  • Coastal and estuarine flows with stratification
  • Hydropower facilities and energy dissipation structures

FS3M

Fluid Structure Sediment Seabed Model Interaction

ü§ù Collaboration with Nakamura Tomoaki

FS3M (Fluid Structure Sediment Seabed Model Interaction) is an advanced modeling system developed in collaboration with Professor Nakamura Tomoaki. This sophisticated software couples fluid dynamics, structural mechanics, sediment transport, and seabed morphology for comprehensive coastal and marine engineering analysis.

Key Capabilities:

  • Multi-Physics Coupling: Fully coupled fluid-structure-sediment-seabed interactions
  • Wave-Structure Interaction: Detailed modeling of wave forces on structures and structural response
  • Sediment Transport: Advanced sediment dynamics including suspended load and bedload
  • Morphodynamics: Long-term seabed evolution and shoreline change prediction
  • Structural Deformation: Elastic and elastoplastic structural response modeling
  • Scour Analysis: Detailed scour prediction around marine structures
  • Liquefaction Assessment: Seabed soil response to wave loading

Applications:

  • Offshore platform design and stability analysis
  • Coastal structure engineering (breakwaters, seawalls, jetties)
  • Pipeline and submarine cable installation
  • Harbor and port facility design
  • Beach nourishment and coastal protection
  • Environmental impact assessment of marine structures

Research Collaboration:

Developed in partnership with Professor Nakamura Tomoaki, a leading researcher in coastal engineering and computational mechanics. This collaboration brings together cutting-edge research with practical engineering applications.
